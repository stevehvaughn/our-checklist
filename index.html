    Weeks 1 – 13

    First Trimester

    The foundational weeks — full of wonder, maybe some nausea, and a lot of important early decisions. Take it one step at a time.

    0/0
    As Soon As You Know
    • Confirm pregnancy with a home test, then a blood test
      hCG blood test confirms and can estimate gestational age
      Medical
    • Choose an OB-GYN or midwife and schedule your first prenatal visit
      Usually around 8–10 weeks; sooner if you have concerns
      Medical
    • Start a prenatal vitamin with folic acid (400–800 mcg)
      Critical for neural tube development in early weeks
      Medical
    • Review your health insurance coverage for maternity care
      Understand your deductible, co-pays, and in-network providers
      Practical
    • Stop alcohol, smoking, and recreational drugs immediately
      Medical
    • Limit caffeine to under 200mg/day (about one 12 oz coffee)
      This is a distinct guideline from alcohol — don't skip it
      Medical
    • Check all current medications with your doctor for safety
      Including OTC drugs and supplements
      Medical
    • Tell your partner — and share this checklist together
      Partner
    • Schedule a dental cleaning
      Pregnancy hormones raise the risk of gum disease; dental care is safe and important
      Medical
    • Learn which foods to avoid
      Deli meat, raw fish, soft unpasteurized cheeses, raw eggs, high-mercury fish
      Medical
    Weeks 6 – 10
    • Attend first prenatal appointment
      Bloodwork, urine test, pelvic exam, medical history
      Medical
    • Hear the heartbeat (first ultrasound ~8 weeks)
      Medical
    • Discuss genetic screening options with your OB
      NIPT, nuchal translucency, carrier screening — know your options
      Medical
    • Research your workplace maternity / paternity leave policies
      Federal FMLA, Colorado FAMLI, and any employer benefits
      Practical
    • Manage morning sickness: ginger, small meals, B6 if severe
      Talk to your OB if you can't keep food down (hyperemesis)
      Medical
    • Begin journaling or documenting this experience
      You'll cherish having it later — even just voice memos count
      Emotional
    • Start Kegel exercises
      Pelvic floor strength aids delivery and dramatically speeds postpartum recovery
      Medical
    Weeks 10 – 13
    • Decide who to tell and when (the "12-week rule" is personal)
      Emotional
    • First trimester screening bloodwork (10–13 weeks)
      Medical
    • Start thinking about birth setting preferences (hospital, birth center)
      Practical
    • Update your will and beneficiaries
      Now is the right time to start thinking about estate basics
      Practical
    • Look into term life insurance if you don't have it
      Practical
    • Check in on each other emotionally — this is big for both of you
      Partner
    Weeks 14 – 27

    Second Trimester

    Often called the "golden trimester" — energy returns, the bump grows, and you'll start feeling those first kicks. There's a lot to prepare and savor.

    0/0
    Weeks 14 – 18
    • Second trimester prenatal appointment (glucose screening begins)
      Medical
    • Quad screen / AFP blood test (15–20 weeks)
      Medical
    • Announce your pregnancy (if you haven't)
      Emotional
    • Notify your employer and HR about your pregnancy
      Start FMLA / FAMLI paperwork conversations early
      Practical
    • Start researching childbirth education classes
      Lamaze, Bradley method, hospital-based — many book up fast
      Practical
    • Begin maternity clothes shopping
      Practical
    • Transition to sleeping on your left side
      Improves blood flow and nutrient delivery to baby; a pregnancy pillow helps
      Medical
    • Begin acclimating pets to baby sounds, smells, and nursery changes
      Play baby sounds, introduce baby-scented items, practice boundaries now
      Practical
    The Big Anatomy Scan (~Week 20)
    • 20-week anatomy ultrasound — the big one!
      Checks all major organ systems; you can often find out the sex
      Medical
    • Decide if you want to know the sex — and if so, plan a reveal
      Emotional
    • Start talking about baby names
      Emotional
    Weeks 20 – 27
    • Research and choose a pediatrician
      Schedule a "meet the doctor" visit before the baby arrives
      Medical
    • Create a baby registry
      Include a range of price points; prioritize safety-rated items
      Baby
    • Research childcare options if returning to work
      Waitlists for daycare can be 6–12+ months long — start now
      Practical
    • Start a baby budget and savings plan
      Estimate first-year costs; look into 529 accounts for education
      Practical
    • 1-hour glucose tolerance test (~24–28 weeks)
      Medical
    • Begin planning the nursery
      Paint fumes — ventilate well and consider low-VOC paints
      Baby
    • Sign up for childbirth education class
      Practical
    • Research and consider hiring a doula
      Proven to reduce labor anxiety and interventions
      Practical
    • Plan a babymoon trip if desired (second trimester is ideal)
      Emotional
    • Talk about parenting values and philosophies together
      Sleep training, feeding choices, screen time — no right answers, just alignment
      Partner
    • Order a breast pump through your insurance
      Most plans cover one 100% — call your insurer or use a pump supplier like Aeroflow
      Practical
    • Schedule maternity photos
      Book now for ~weeks 28–34 — the sweet spot before you're too uncomfortable
      Emotional
    • Learn how to track fetal kick counts
      Starting ~week 28, aim for 10 movements in 2 hours; call your OB if activity drops
      Medical
    Weeks 28 – 40

    Third Trimester

    The home stretch. Things get real — and wonderful and uncomfortable all at once. Get your nest ready and trust the process.

    0/0
    Weeks 28 – 32
    • Tdap vaccination (~27–36 weeks)
      Protects baby against whooping cough before they can be vaccinated
      Medical
    • RhoGAM shot if Rh-negative
      Medical
    • Complete childbirth education class
      Practical
    • Take a newborn care class
      Covers bathing, feeding, swaddling, safe sleep
      Baby
    • Take a breastfeeding / infant feeding class
      Baby
    • Buy and install the infant car seat — get it inspected
      Many fire stations offer free inspections
      Baby
    • Finalize and set up the nursery
      Safe sleep: firm mattress, no bumpers/pillows/blankets in crib
      Baby
    • Baby shower — enjoy it!
      Emotional
    • File your FMLA / short-term disability paperwork
      Don't just notify HR — submit the actual forms; timing matters for benefit eligibility
      Practical
    Weeks 32 – 36
    • Pre-register at your hospital or birth center
      Practical
    • Tour your birth facility
      Practical
    • Wash and organize all baby clothes and gear
      Use fragrance-free, baby-safe detergent
      Baby
    • Stock the house: diapers, wipes, formula or nursing supplies
      Practical
    • Research cord blood banking — decide yes or no
      Medical
    • Group B Strep test (~35–37 weeks)
      Medical
    • Freeze meals for after baby arrives (freezer meals!)
      Double every recipe you cook and freeze the second batch
      Practical
    • Add baby to your health insurance plan (within 30 days of birth)
      You'll need to do this immediately after birth — know the process now
      Practical
    • Take a newborn CPR and infant first aid class
      One of the most valuable things you can do before baby arrives — many hospitals offer free classes
      Practical
    Weeks 36 – 40
    • Pack your hospital bag
      For mom, partner, and baby — don't forget phone chargers
      Practical
    • Know the signs of labor and when to go to the hospital
      5-1-1 rule: contractions 5 min apart, lasting 1 min, for 1 hour
      Medical
    • Finalize your support network — who is your "call list"?
      Practical
    • Plan for pet care and home coverage while at the hospital
      Practical
    • Have a date night (or a quiet night in) — savor the two of you
      Partner
    • Begin daily perineal massage
      Starting at week 36, research shows it reduces tearing and recovery time at delivery
      Medical
    • Rest. Seriously. Sleep while you still can.
      Emotional
    Before & During Birth

    Birth Preparation

    Know your preferences, communicate them clearly, and remember: flexibility is part of the plan. Whatever happens, you've got this.

    0/0
    Birth Plan
    • Write your birth plan and discuss with your OB
      Keep it flexible — 1–2 pages max, no doctor wants an essay
      Practical
    • Decide your pain management preferences (epidural, natural, etc.)
      Medical
    • Decide on delayed cord clamping preferences
      Medical
    • Decide on skin-to-skin contact immediately after birth
      Baby
    • Decide on feeding preferences (breast, formula, or both) — no judgment
      Baby
    • Discuss who will be in the delivery room
      Partner
    • Create a music playlist for labor
      Emotional
    • Decide on vitamin K injection and antibiotic eye ointment for newborn
      Both are standard at birth; discuss with your OB if you have questions or preferences
      Medical
    Hospital Bag Essentials
    • ID, insurance cards, and hospital pre-registration paperwork
      Practical
    • Comfortable clothes and toiletries for mom (2–3 day stay)
      Practical
    • Snacks and comfort items for partner
      Partner
    • Newborn outfit and swaddle blanket for going home
      Baby
    • Phone + charger + camera
      Practical
    • Nursing bra and nipple cream (if breastfeeding)
      Practical
    • Car seat installed and ready in the car
      Baby
    At the Hospital
    • Complete birth certificate paperwork at the hospital
      Practical
    • Apply for Social Security number at the hospital (if offered)
      Practical
    • Ask about lactation consultant support before discharge
      Medical
    • Take all the free samples, diapers, and formula they offer
      Seriously — take everything
      Practical
    After Baby Arrives

    Postpartum

    The fourth trimester is real. Take care of yourselves, accept help, and know that the fog eventually lifts. You're doing better than you think.

    0/0
    First Two Weeks Home
    • Schedule newborn's first pediatrician visit (24–72 hours after discharge)
      Medical
    • Set up a feeding log (app or paper) — wet/dirty diapers, feeds, sleep
      Sleep deprivation makes memory unreliable; log everything
      Baby
    • Accept all help offered — meals, cleaning, errands
      Say yes. Now is not the time for pride.
      Emotional
    • Watch for signs of postpartum depression or anxiety (both partners)
      PPD affects 1 in 5 moms — and 1 in 10 dads. Not a weakness.
      Emotional
    • Add baby to health insurance (do this within 30 days of birth!)
      Practical
    • Register the birth certificate with your county/state
      Practical
    • Mom: care for your body — rest, peri bottle, stool softener, pads
      Recovery from birth (vaginal or C-section) takes time and care
      Medical
    • Partner: take your leave — be fully present these first weeks
      Partner
    • Set up a meal train for the first few weeks home
      Apps like MealTrain or Take Them a Meal make it easy to coordinate friends and family
      Practical
    Weeks 2 – 6
    • Ongoing pediatrician well-baby visits (2 weeks, 1 month…)
      Medical
    • Mom's 6-week postpartum OB checkup
      Physical recovery, mental health screening, contraception discussion
      Medical
    • Connect with a lactation consultant if breastfeeding challenges arise
      Medical
    • Find a new parent support group or mommy & me class
      Community is one of the most underrated postpartum tools
      Emotional
    • Update your will to include your child
      Practical
    • Open a 529 college savings account
      Practical
    • Check in with each other as a couple — not just as parents
      It's easy to lose the relationship in the chaos of a newborn
      Partner
    • Plan your return to work and childcare logistics
      Practical
    • Take a hundred photos. Then take a hundred more.
      Emotional
    • Schedule a postpartum pelvic floor physical therapy appointment
      Now recommended for all new moms — helps with recovery, leaking, and core healing
      Medical
